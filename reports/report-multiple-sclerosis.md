**Druggability:** NR3C1 is a highly druggable target, belonging to a well-established drug class (nuclear hormone receptors) with numerous 3D structures available. The existence of 27 known ligands, including several compounds that have reached Phase 4 clinical development, confirms its excellent tractability for therapeutic intervention.

**Literature:** Several studies show conflicting results regarding the role of NR3C1 in multiple sclerosis (MS). Some research indicates that specific NR3C1 gene polymorphisms (e.g., rs6189, rs41423247) and altered NR3C1 expression are associated with an increased risk of MS development or its molecular etiology. However, other studies did not find an association between different NR3C1 polymorphisms and MS disease severity, clinical improvement, or response to glucocorticoid treatment in various patient cohorts.

## 2. IL2RA — interleukin 2 receptor subunit alpha

| UniProt Accession | P01589 |
| Protein Class | Unknown |
| 3D Structure | Yes |
| Known Compounds | 1 |
| Max Drug Phase | 0 |
| PubMed Papers | 90 |
| Evidence | supporting |

**Druggability:** IL2RA is a druggable target due to its membrane receptor nature, high therapeutic relevance in immune regulation, and availability of multiple 3D structures. While small molecule progress appears limited (1 compound, phase 0), its role as a receptor makes it amenable to various therapeutic modalities, including antibodies.

**Literature:** Multiple studies, including large meta-analyses, consistently demonstrate that polymorphisms in the IL2RA gene, specifically rs2104286 and rs12722489, are associated with an increased risk of multiple sclerosis (MS) in both Caucasian and Asian populations. These MS-associated IL2RA SNPs have been shown to influence CD25 expression on CD4+ T cell subsets, providing a potential mechanism for their contribution to MS susceptibility.

**PMIDs:** 26718593, 40086235, 29648897

**Druggability:** TNFRSF1A is a highly druggable target due to its cell membrane/secreted receptor nature, abundant 3D structural data, and the existence of 100 known compounds, including one that has reached Phase 2 clinical trials.

**Literature:** Polymorphisms and mutations in the TNFRSF1A gene, particularly rs1800693 and the R92Q variant, are consistently associated with an increased susceptibility to multiple sclerosis (MS) in various populations, including childhood-onset MS. The rs1800693 C allele specifically shows an independent association with MS risk. While some TNFRSF1A variants are linked to MS susceptibility and pathogenesis, their direct influence on disease severity, age at onset, or disability progression is not consistently established.

**PMIDs:** 28927886, 30009568, 24174591

## 4. KEAP1 — kelch like ECH associated protein 1

| Protein Class | Belongs to the KEAP1 family |
| 3D Structure | Yes |
| Known Compounds | 93 |
| Max Drug Phase | 0 |
| PubMed Papers | 58 |
| Evidence | supporting |

**Druggability:** KEAP1 is a highly tractable enzyme target, supported by numerous 3D structures and known early-stage small molecule modulators that leverage its covalent modification mechanism. While its intracellular localization and absence of clinical-phase compounds highlight ongoing optimization challenges, its critical role in oxidative stress makes it therapeutically significant.

**Literature:** The KEAP1-Nrf2-ARE pathway is a critical regulator of oxidative stress and inflammation, central to multiple sclerosis (MS) pathophysiology. Inhibiting KEAP1, which stabilizes Nrf2, is a validated therapeutic strategy for MS, with dimethyl fumarate (DMF) being an FDA-approved first-line oral drug. Additionally, KEAP1 has been identified as a shared genetic risk factor between MS and hospitalized COVID-19, further underscoring its relevance in MS.

| Metric | Value |

| Protein Class | Belongs to the MS4A family |
| 3D Structure | Yes |
| Known Compounds | 0 |
| Max Drug Phase | 0 |

| Evidence | supporting |

**Druggability:** MS4A1 exhibits good inherent druggability potential due to its cell membrane localization and numerous available 3D structures, although the current absence of known bioactive compounds or clinical progress suggests it may be an underexplored target.

**Literature:** MS4A1 (CD20) is consistently implicated in multiple sclerosis (MS) pathogenesis and as a therapeutic target. It is found upregulated in brain tissue-resident memory T cells and enriched on a distinct NK cell population in MS patients. Elevated MS4A1 levels are observed in CSF-derived extracellular vesicles in MS, correlating with disease, and its expression is modulated by MS therapies like IFN-beta-1b and directly targeted by ocrelizumab, highlighting its central role in the disease's immunopathology.

**PMIDs:** 36594029, 40849886, 40680248

## Recommendation

**Recommendation:**

KEAP1 represents the most compelling drug target for multiple sclerosis, demonstrating strong association and an already validated therapeutic strategy through FDA-approved dimethyl fumarate. IL2RA, TNFRSF1A, and MS4A1 also emerge as highly promising, supported by consistent genetic associations and established roles in MS immunopathology, with MS4A1 (CD20) already targeted by ocrelizumab. The primary risk with NR3C1 is the conflicting literature regarding its role in MS despite its excellent druggability, demanding further mechanistic validation before prioritization. For IL2RA and MS4A1, future efforts should explore diverse therapeutic modalities, including biologics and novel small molecule scaffolds, given the current limited small molecule progress. Overall, prioritizing targets with robust evidence and existing therapeutic pathways will accelerate MS drug discovery.
